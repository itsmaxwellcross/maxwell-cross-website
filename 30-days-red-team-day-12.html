30 Days of Red Team: Day 12 — Data Exfiltration

Maxwell Cross • Dec 16, 2025

Access means nothing if you can't get the data out.

You have Domain Admin. You have the persistence. You have the Crown Jewels.

But if you try to drag-and-drop 50GB of data across the network, you will trigger every alarm in the Security Operations Center (SOC).

Amateurs think exfiltration is about speed. Professionals know it is about Invisibility.

Today, we learn how to move data past Firewalls, IDS, and Data Loss Prevention (DLP) systems by breaking it into microscopic chunks and hiding it where defenders never look.

A Glimpse of the Code: Steganography

DLP systems scan for keywords like "Confidential" or file types like .sql. They don't look inside your vacation photos.

In Day 12, we build a Steganography Toolkit that hides sensitive data inside the pixels of an image using Least Significant Bit (LSB) encoding.

Here is the logic that turns a JPEG into a data mule:
