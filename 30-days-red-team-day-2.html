30 Days of Red Team: Day 2 — Weaponizing OSINT

Maxwell Cross • Jan 23, 2026

If Day 1 was learning to walk, Day 2 is learning to run.

Most defenders focus on firewalls and intrusion detection systems. But attackers? We are building detailed maps of their infrastructure using nothing but public information.

Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) is the art of finding the data companies don't even realize they are exposing.

Every time a company posts a job listing, updates their SSL certificate, or pushes code to GitHub, they are handing us ammunition. Today, we automate the process of collecting it.

A Glimpse of the Code: Automated Google Dorking

Google is the most powerful hacking tool ever created, but manual searching is too slow. In Day 2, we build a tool to automate "Dorking"—using search operators to find sensitive files.

Here is the core logic from the Auto-Dorker we build in the full article:

def build_dork(self, dork_type):
    """
    Build targeted queries for different reconnaissance objectives
    """
    dorks = {
        'subdomains': f'site:*.{self.domain} -site:www.{self.domain}',
        'files': f'site:{self.domain} (ext:pdf OR ext:doc OR ext:xls)',
        'logins': f'site:{self.domain} (inurl:login OR inurl:admin)',
        'configs': f'site:{self.domain} (ext:xml OR ext:conf OR ext:cfg)',
        'backup_files': f'site:{self.domain} (ext:bkf OR ext:bak OR ext:old)',
        'credentials': f'site:{self.domain} (password OR passwd OR pwd)'
    }
    return dorks.get(dork_type, '')

I once used a script like this to find a database backup file (.bak) indexed by Google for a major financial institution. It contained admin credentials. I never even had to touch their server.
