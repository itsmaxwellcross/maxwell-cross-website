30 Days of Red Team: Day 15.5 — The Mid-Point Review

Maxwell Cross • Jan 12, 2026

Welcome back, operators.

I know what you’re thinking: “Where the hell have you been?”

Fair question.

Here’s the truth: even red teamers unplug during the holidays. We spend time with family, eat too much food, and pretend we’re not mentally mapping the security posture of our relatives’ home networks. It’s called balance. Look it up.

But holidays are over. The eggnog is gone. The decorations are packed away. And now? Now we get back to work.

And by “back to work,” I mean we’re about to drop a rapid-fire series of articles to catch up on the schedule. Think of it as a post-holiday content blitz. You’re about to get more red team knowledge in the next two weeks than most people learn in six months of “cybersecurity bootcamps.”

But before we dive into Day 16 proper, let’s take a breath.

Let’s look at where we’ve been. Let’s look at where we’re going. And let’s make sure you’re ready for what comes next.

Because if you thought the first 15 days were intense, you’re not ready for the second half of this series.

Where We’ve Been: Days 1–15 Recap

Let’s rewind.

Fifteen days ago, you started with nothing. No methodology. No framework. No understanding of how real attackers operate. You were standing at the beginning of the kill chain with a laptop and determination.

Today? You understand the entire attack lifecycle. You have tools. You have techniques. You have the mindset of an operator.

Here’s the journey we’ve taken together:

Week 1: Reconnaissance & Initial Access (Days 1–7)

We started at the beginning: understanding how attackers think and operate.

✅ Day 1: Understanding the Cyber Kill Chain — You learned the framework that governs every successful cyber operation. Reconnaissance, weaponization, delivery, exploitation, installation, C2, actions on objectives. You learned that red team operations aren’t random — they’re systematic.

✅ Day 2: Advanced OSINT & External Reconnaissance — You learned to gather intelligence without ever touching the target network. LinkedIn scraping, domain enumeration, technology profiling, email harvesting. You learned that reconnaissance is 80% of the operation.

✅ Day 3: Weaponization & Payload Development — You learned to craft payloads that evade detection. Shellcode generation, payload encoding, AV bypass techniques, polymorphic payloads. You learned that weaponization is an art, not a science.

✅ Day 4: Social Engineering & Delivery Mechanisms — You learned to weaponize human psychology. Phishing campaigns, pretexting, credential harvesting, landing pages. You learned that the weakest link in any security chain is the human behind the keyboard.

✅ Day 5: Initial Exploitation Techniques — You learned to get your first shell. Exploit frameworks, vulnerability exploitation, callback handling, staged vs. stageless payloads. You learned that initial access is just the beginning.

✅ Day 6: Post-Exploitation & Situational Awareness — You learned what to do immediately after getting access. System enumeration, user context assessment, network positioning, privilege identification. You learned that the first 15 minutes after exploitation determine success or failure.

✅ Day 7: Week 1 Integration & Practice — You synthesized everything from Week 1 into a complete operation. From reconnaissance to post-exploitation. You learned that theory means nothing without practice.

The Result?

You went from zero understanding to complete initial access operations. You know how to find targets, craft payloads, deliver them, exploit vulnerabilities, and establish presence.

Week 2: Persistence & Command Control (Days 8–14)

But access means nothing if you can’t keep it.

Week 2 was about survival and communication. You learned to persist on compromised systems and maintain command and control even when defenders are hunting you.

✅ Day 8: Windows Persistence Mechanisms — You learned every technique to survive on Windows. Registry Run keys, scheduled tasks, startup folders, WMI event subscriptions, service installations. You learned that persistence isn’t about one technique — it’s about redundancy.

✅ Day 9: Linux Persistence Techniques — You adapted your persistence toolkit for Linux environments. Cron jobs, systemd services, bashrc modifications, SSH key injection, library preloading. You learned that Linux persistence requires precision and stealth.

✅ Day 10: Building Custom C2 Infrastructure — You learned to build command and control from scratch. Server setup, listener configuration, handler development, communication protocols. You learned that C2 is the nervous system of any red team operation.

✅ Day 11: C2 Communication Channels — You learned to communicate without detection. HTTP/S beaconing, DNS tunneling, ICMP covert channels, domain fronting. You learned that modern C2 isn’t about hiding — it’s about blending into normal traffic.

✅ Day 12: Data Exfiltration Methods — You learned to steal data without triggering alarms. Bandwidth throttling, encrypted channels, cloud storage abuse, steganography, protocol smuggling. You learned that exfiltration is about patience, not speed.

✅ Day 13: Operational Security & Anti-Forensics — You learned to cover your tracks. Log manipulation, timestamp modification, artifact removal, memory-only execution, secure deletion. You learned that the best operations are the ones defenders never see.

✅ Day 14: Week 2 Integration & Practice — You combined persistence, C2, exfiltration, and anti-forensics into complete operational security. You learned that surviving on a network requires orchestrating multiple techniques simultaneously.

The Result?

You’re not just inside anymore. You’re entrenched. You have persistence across multiple systems. You have reliable C2 communications. You can exfiltrate data. You can cover your tracks. You’re invisible.

Week 3: Lateral Movement & Privilege Escalation (Days 15)

Week 3 is where everything changes.

This is where you learn to escalate privileges and move laterally through the network. This is where single-system compromise becomes network-wide dominance.

✅ Day 15: Credential Harvesting — You learned to extract every credential stored on compromised systems. LSASS memory dumping, SAM database extraction, registry credential theft, browser password harvesting. You learned that credentials are the keys to the kingdom.

Currently In Progress…

You’ve completed the foundation. You understand initial access, persistence, and C2. You know how to operate without detection. You’ve just learned to harvest credentials.

Now comes the hard part: escalation and lateral movement.

What You Should Have By Now

Let’s do an inventory check.

If you’ve been following along, here’s what you should have in your red team toolkit after 15 days:

Week 1 Skills — Reconnaissance & Initial Access:

  • Complete understanding of the Cyber Kill Chain framework
  • OSINT collection tools and methodologies
  • Payload development and weaponization techniques
  • Social engineering infrastructure (phishing, pretexting, credential harvesting)
  • Initial exploitation capabilities
  • Post-exploitation enumeration and situational awareness procedures

Week 2 Skills — Persistence & Command Control:

  • Windows persistence mechanisms (10+ techniques)
  • Linux persistence techniques (10+ techniques)
  • Custom C2 infrastructure
  • Multiple C2 communication channels (HTTP/S, DNS, ICMP)
  • Data exfiltration methods across multiple protocols
  • Operational security and anti-forensics capabilities

Week 3 Skills — Credential Harvesting:

  • LSASS memory dumping tools
  • SAM database extraction techniques
  • Registry credential harvesting
  • Browser password extraction
  • Credential processing and cracking capabilities

If you’re missing any of these, go back. Seriously. The second half of Week 3 and all of Week 4 build on everything we’ve done so far.

Where We’re Going: Days 16–30 Preview

Alright, let’s talk about what’s coming.

The first 15 days were about getting in, staying in, and stealing credentials.

The next 15 days are about complete network compromise and domain dominance.

Here’s what’s coming:

Week 3 (Continued): Lateral Movement & Privilege Escalation (Days 16–21)

  • Day 16: Windows Privilege Escalation — Tomorrow, you’ll learn to escalate from user to admin to SYSTEM. Unquoted service paths, weak permissions, DLL hijacking, token manipulation, UAC bypasses. You’ll learn that Windows privilege escalation is a science.
  • Day 17: Linux Privilege Escalation — You’ll adapt your escalation skills to Linux. SUID binaries, kernel exploits, sudo misconfigurations, capabilities abuse, cron exploitation. You’ll learn that Linux escalation rewards patience and precision.
  • Day 18: Lateral Movement Techniques — You’ll learn to move through networks like a ghost. Pass-the-Hash, WMI execution, PSRemoting, DCOM exploitation. You’ll learn that lateral movement is about stealth, not noise.
  • Day 19: Pivoting & Network Tunneling — You’ll learn to reach previously unreachable networks. SSH tunneling, SOCKS proxies, port forwarding, network segmentation bypass. You’ll learn that pivoting turns isolated systems into network highways.
  • Day 20: Exploiting Trust Relationships — You’ll learn to abuse the trust that makes enterprise networks function. Forest trusts, domain trusts, SQL Server links, certificate authorities. You’ll learn that trust is the weakest link in enterprise security.
  • Day 21: Week 3 Integration & Practice — You’ll combine credential harvesting, privilege escalation, lateral movement, and pivoting into complete network compromise operations. You’ll learn to think like an APT operator.

Week 4: Active Directory & Domain Dominance (Days 22–28)

This is where everything we’ve built leads to.

  • Day 22: Active Directory Enumeration — You’ll learn to map Active Directory like a professional. Users, groups, computers, OUs, GPOs, ACLs, trusts. You’ll learn that AD enumeration reveals the entire enterprise architecture.
  • Day 23: Kerberoasting & AS-REP Roasting — You’ll learn to extract and crack service account credentials. Kerberoasting attacks, AS-REP roasting, targeted SPN hunting. You’ll learn that Kerberos authentication is a credential goldmine.
  • Day 24: Pass-the-Ticket & Overpass-the-Hash — You’ll learn to move laterally without passwords. TGT extraction, TGT injection, RC4 to AES conversion. You’ll learn that Kerberos tickets are better than passwords.
  • Day 25: Golden & Silver Tickets — You’ll learn to forge Kerberos tickets. Golden Ticket attacks for domain persistence, Silver Ticket attacks for service access. You’ll learn that with the right key, you can impersonate anyone.
  • Day 26: DCSync & Domain Admin Compromise — You’ll learn to steal every credential in the domain. DCSync attacks, NTDS.dit extraction, replication abuse. You’ll learn that Domain Admin isn’t the goal — it’s the beginning of total control.
  • Day 27: Persistence at Domain Level — You’ll learn to maintain access at the domain level. AdminSDHolder abuse, DCShadow, Skeleton Keys, DSRM backdoors. You’ll learn that domain-level persistence is invisible and nearly impossible to remove.
  • Day 28: Week 4 Integration & Practice — You’ll orchestrate complete domain compromise operations from reconnaissance to domain admin to persistence. You’ll learn to operate like an advanced persistent threat.

Week 5: Advanced Topics & Wrap-Up (Days 29–30)

  • Day 29: Advanced Evasion & Detection Bypass — You’ll learn to evade modern security controls. EDR bypass, AMSI evasion, logging disruption, behavioral detection avoidance. You’ll learn that the best operators know exactly how defenders see them.
  • Day 30: Series Wrap-Up & Building Your Career — You’ll learn what comes next. Certifications, career paths, continued learning, portfolio building, professional development. You’ll learn that Day 30 isn’t the end — it’s the beginning of your red team career.

The Second Half Gets Real

Let me be honest with you.

The first two weeks were designed to build your foundation. They were challenging, but they were structured. We built tools, we tested them, we learned the basics of operations.

Week 3 is where things change.

Week 3 assumes you have the foundation. You understand how to gain access, maintain persistence, and operate with stealth. Now we escalate. Now we move laterally. Now we compromise entire networks.

If you struggled with Weeks 1–2, now is the time to catch up. Go back. Review. Practice. Test your tools in a lab environment.

If you crushed Weeks 1–2, you’re ready. Week 3 will push you into territory most penetration testers never reach. You’ll learn techniques that separate professionals from script kiddies.

Week 4 — Active Directory Dominance — is where legends are made.

By Day 28, you’ll understand Active Directory attacks at a level that makes you dangerous. You’ll know how to enumerate, attack, and persist in AD environments. You’ll understand Kerberos exploitation, ticket forgery, and domain compromise.

Week 5 wraps it all together with advanced evasion and career guidance.

What’s Next: Rapid-Fire Content Schedule

Here’s the plan:

Over the next two weeks, I’ll be posting back-to-back articles to catch up on the schedule. Expect 3–4 articles per week until we’re back on track.

Each article will maintain the same depth, detail, and quality you’ve come to expect. I’m not rushing the content — I’m just accelerating the release schedule.

The schedule:

  • Day 16: Windows Privilege Escalation (drops tomorrow)
  • Day 17: Linux Privilege Escalation
  • Day 18: Lateral Movement Techniques
  • Day 19: Pivoting & Network Tunneling
  • Day 20: Exploiting Trust Relationships
  • Day 21: Week 3 Integration & Practice

Then we hit Week 4 — Active Directory week. This is what everything has been building toward.

A Note on Ethics (Because It Matters)

Before we continue, let’s be clear:

Everything in this series is designed for authorized security testing only.

You should be testing these techniques in:

  • Your own lab environment
  • Environments you have explicit written permission to test
  • Red team engagements with signed contracts and rules of engagement

If you use these techniques without authorization, you’re not a red teamer. You’re a criminal. And you’ll be treated as one.

The goal of this series is to create skilled, ethical security professionals who can think like attackers to better defend organizations. If that’s not your goal, this series isn’t for you.

Final Thoughts: The Journey Continues

Fifteen days ago, you started this journey with nothing.

Today, you have a toolkit that most penetration testers would envy. You understand the complete attack lifecycle. You have the mindset of an operator.

But we’re only halfway done.

The next 15 days will take everything you’ve learned and push it further. You’ll learn to escalate privileges on any system. You’ll move laterally through networks invisibly. You’ll compromise entire Active Directory domains.

By Day 30, you’ll have completed one of the most comprehensive red team education programs ever published publicly.

But only if you keep going.

The holiday break is over. The foundation phase is over. The real work begins now.

Homework Before Day 16

Before we dive into Day 16, make sure you have:

✅ Reviewed Days 1–15 — Go back through the articles. Make sure you understand the concepts. Test your tools.

✅ Set up a Windows lab environment — Days 16–21 focus heavily on Windows and AD. You’ll need a lab with at least 2–3 Windows machines. Bonus: Set up a domain controller for Week 4 prep.

✅ Organized your toolkit — Put all your scripts, tools, and notes in one place. Create a structured directory for your red team operations.

✅ Prepared for acceleration — The next two weeks will be intense. Block out time to read, practice, and implement. This is going to be a sprint to catch up.

✅ Got your credentials from Day 15 ready — The credentials you harvested on Day 15 are the foundation for privilege escalation on Day 16. Make sure you have working credential harvesting tools.

What’s Next: Day 16 Tomorrow

Tomorrow, we escalate.

Day 16: Windows Privilege Escalation

You’ll learn to escalate from low-privileged user to SYSTEM on Windows:

  • Unquoted service paths and weak permissions exploitation
  • DLL hijacking and search order abuse
  • Token manipulation and privilege escalation
  • UAC bypass techniques
  • AlwaysInstallElevated and other misconfigurations
  • Automated privilege escalation enumeration tools
  • Building your own privilege escalation framework

You’ll build tools that systematically identify and exploit every common Windows privilege escalation vector. You’ll learn to combine credential harvesting with privilege escalation into complete system compromise.

This is where the credentials you harvested on Day 15 become domain admin access.

This is where you stop being a user and become SYSTEM.

Day 16 drops tomorrow.

Be ready.
